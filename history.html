The Beginning

STARTED IN A BASEMENT.

Ironform was founded in 2011 by Marcus Webb — a former collegiate wrestler who couldn't find a gym that matched his intensity. With a 600 sq ft basement, a squat rack, and a single Olympic barbell, he opened his doors to five members and a promise: no shortcuts, no excuses.

Word spread. Within eighteen months, that basement had a waiting list. By 2013, Marcus had signed a lease on a 6,000 sq ft warehouse space in the Meatpacking District, and Ironform as the city knows it today was born.

Today, Ironform spans three floors, hosts 30+ weekly classes, and serves over 4,000 active members — but the philosophy hasn't changed a bit.

MILESTONES

2011

THE BASEMENT OPENS

Marcus Webb opens Ironform out of a 600 sq ft basement with five founding members. Cash-only, no frills, maximum effort.

2013

WAREHOUSE MOVE

Ironform relocates to a 6,000 sq ft warehouse in the Meatpacking District. Full equipment buildout, five coaches, and the first group class program launches.

2015

COMPETITION TEAM

The Ironform Competition Team is formed. In its first year, members place at USPA Nationals in powerlifting and the CrossFit Open Regional.

2018

THIRD FLOOR EXPANSION

We double in size, adding a dedicated cardio + mobility floor, recovery lounge, and sports therapy suite — the first of its kind in our district.

2021

10 YEAR ANNIVERSARY

A decade in. We host a 24-hour charity workout event raising $40,000 for youth athletics programs across New York City. 800 members participate.

2023

STILL BUILDING.

4,000 members, 18 coaches, and a community stronger than ever. The mission hasn't changed since day one: show up, do the work, grow.
